Albert Einstein

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia

Albert Einstein was a theoretical physicist widely acknowledged to be one of the greatest physicists of all time.

He is best known for developing the theory of relativity, but he also made important contributions to the development of the theory of quantum mechanics.

Einstein was born in Ulm, Kingdom of Württemberg, in the G.E. on 14 March 1879.

He published more than 300 scientific papers and over 150 non-scientific works. His intellectual achievements and originality have made the word Einstein synonymous with genius.

Education

He studied at the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zurich, graduating in 1900 with a degree in Mathematics and Physics.

Famous Equation

Einstein developed the mass–energy equivalence formula: E = mc2.

Later Years

He moved to the United States in 1933 and took a position at the Institute for Advanced Study in Princeton, New Jersey.

"Imagination is more important than knowledge. For knowledge is limited." – Albert Einstein

Scientific Contributions

  • Photoelectric effect
  • General relativity
  • Special relativity
  • Brownian motion

Mathematical Expression

E = mc²
Where:
E = energy
m = mass
c = speed of light

He lived in Princeton until his death in 1955. His brain was preserved for research, though this has led to some controversypublic debate.

Einstein's name is associated with many achievements in science, and he received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1921 for his explanation of the photoelectric effect.
